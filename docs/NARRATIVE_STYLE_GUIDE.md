# Narrative Style Guide

## 1. Honest Status

The current seed stories are **not yet full cinematic narratives**. They are structured story records with summaries, kid summaries, SEO/AEO fields, and linkages.

We have not created full Mahagatha-style narratives from the uploaded book because modern books are copyrighted unless we have rights. The right approach is:

- use Mahagatha as inspiration for topic selection and market taste,
- build original retellings from source notes,
- cite public-domain/permissioned sources,
- avoid paraphrasing a modern book chapter-by-chapter.

## 2. Narrative Standard

Every cinematic story should feel like:

- a premium documentary narration,
- a mythic film sequence,
- emotionally clear,
- source-aware,
- devotional without becoming careless,
- dramatic without inventing unsupported facts.

Target reading/listening experience:

- adults should feel the grandeur,
- children should understand the emotional arc,
- parents should trust the source care,
- search engines should understand the entities and answers.

## 3. Required Structure

### Story Pack

Each full story should contain:

1. `source_brief`
   - exact source references,
   - major variants,
   - what is known,
   - what is later tradition,
   - what we are choosing to tell.

2. `one_line_hook`
   - cinematic entry point.

3. `direct_answer`
   - 40-word AEO answer.

4. `adult_cinematic_narrative`
   - 1,200-2,500 words depending on importance.

5. `kids_narrative_5_8`
   - gentle, simple, visual.

6. `kids_narrative_9_12`
   - richer but still safe.

7. `voiceover_script`
   - cinematic pacing,
   - SSML breaks,
   - tone markers,
   - pronunciation notes.

8. `booklet_chapters`
   - cover line,
   - chapter spreads,
   - talapatra inserts,
   - reflection page,
   - source page.

9. `image_prompts`
   - hero image,
   - character sheet,
   - scene images,
   - coloring pages.

10. `activity_pack`
   - quiz,
   - drawing prompt,
   - parent question,
   - competition idea.

## 4. Cinematic Arc Template

Use this structure:

1. **Opening Image**
   - One vivid moment.
   - Example: "A king looked into the sky and saw famine written among the stars."

2. **World Before**
   - Establish kingdom, forest, heaven, temple, or family.

3. **Disturbance**
   - A vow, curse, boon, birth, exile, warning, or cosmic movement.

4. **Moral Pressure**
   - What dharma demands.
   - What the character fears losing.

5. **Action**
   - The decisive choice.

6. **Encounter**
   - Deity, sage, enemy, teacher, child, or cosmic force.

7. **Reversal**
   - The story turns; power becomes humility, fear becomes understanding.

8. **Resolution**
   - The event concludes.

9. **Meaning**
   - What the story teaches without sounding like a school moral.

10. **Living Link**
   - How it connects to festivals, temples, rituals, family life, or modern choices.

## 5. Voiceover Style

Your Shani script is the right direction:

- short lines,
- strong pauses,
- repeated motifs,
- direct emotional framing,
- chapter markers,
- reverence,
- consequence,
- clarity.

Improve it with:

- explicit source note at start/end,
- variant note where needed,
- pronunciation glossary,
- fewer unsupported absolutes,
- one parent-friendly meaning section,
- one child-safe recap.

## 6. Example Output Shape: Dasharatha Meets Shani

### Hook

A king looked into the sky and saw hunger approaching Earth.

### Direct Answer

In a Puranic Shani tradition, King Dasharatha confronts Shani to prevent a feared drought linked with Rohini. Shani is moved by the king's courage and grants protection to those who worship with humility and sincerity.

### Chapter Plan

1. The Sky Warning
2. A King's Duty
3. The Road Beyond the Sun
4. Standing Before Shani
5. The Boon
6. What Karma Teaches

### Booklet Talapatra Inserts

- "Courage is not the absence of fear; it is fear placed in service of others."
- "Shani does not hurry, because karma does not hurry."
- "A king protects not only land, but the breath of his people."

### Kid Version Lesson

Sometimes being brave means speaking up before others are hurt.

## 7. Language Rules

Use:

- Rama, Sita, Shiva, Shani in readable English.
- Store diacritics optionally in fields like `nameSanskrit` or `transliteration`.
- Avoid cluttering kid pages with too many diacritics.
- Use Sanskrit terms with short meanings.

Avoid:

- fake ancient tone,
- excessive violence for children,
- unsupported "this is the only truth" claims,
- copying modern book phrasing,
- flattening complex stories into generic morals.

## 8. Review Checklist

Before publication:

- source reference exists,
- variants are acknowledged where needed,
- no modern copyrighted phrasing,
- child-safe adaptation exists,
- KG links are present,
- AEO answer exists,
- quiz/activity exists,
- image prompts do not distort sacred identities,
- audio script has pronunciation notes.
